Introduction

Problem

When Healthicity first created it's Compliance Manager application, we were under an extremely tight deadline to deliver the finished product. Because of this, may of the modules, such as the Document Center, were created with an minimum approach. Because of this, we did not have time to user test the document center or interview users on what they needed from this module.

In fact, most of the users didn't even use the Document Center because it couldn't do what they needed it to do.

After the launch of Compliance Manager, we conducted research around usability with each of the modules to determine which of them needed the most work. Based on the initial research, the Document Center had the lowest of the usability scores at 18 (out of 100). Not only did it user find the interface intuitive, they were unable to accomplish the goals they needed with the module. In fact, most of the users didn't even use the Document Center because it couldn't do what they needed it to do.

Initial Design

Research

After conducting additional User Research, we determined that we needed to create a system that could not only organize the companies' compliance documents, but give them the ability to manage permissions, generate PDFs, maintain a version history.

The users needed all of these features in a way that was easy to navigate and easy to find the documents they were looking for.
