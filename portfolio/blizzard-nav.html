Updated Navigation

Problem:

During post-launch usability sessions with the Blizzard shop redesign, I noticed users were having problems with the new navigation during certain scenarios as well as problems with the overall discoverability of the new navigation. Through analytics, we noticed a drop off in conversions from users using the global navigation.

Solution:

  1. Make it easier to reach the Shop from Game Sites
  2. Make the Shop Navigation easier to find
  3. Simplify cross-franchise and cross-product navigation

Overview:

When we began usability testing on the Blizzard shop, I focused on what I thought was the most likely scenarios. However, after launch, we discovered through analytics and user research that there were a few cases that we had missed and were causing issues. We took a holistic approach to the navigation and went back to the drawing board to find out how users were using the shop and the journey they were taking to get there.

I found that most of our users were navigation to the shop from one of our game-sites and there wasn’t a consistently easy path to the shop from them. We also found that many of our users were actually browsing through multiple products and franchises within the same session and we hadn’t provided a clear cut way to do this. Lastly, a large portion of our users were not able to find the shop navigation easily.

Final Designs

The designs below were the final mocks before moving to the build phase of the project. During the build phase, some changes needed to be made because of technical and time constraints. You can see the most current version of the Blizzard Shop at shop.blizzard.com.

Solution 1: Make it easier for users to reach the shop from game sites by adding in a shop link in each of the game site’s navigation

Solution 2: Make the shop navigation easier to find through visual design updates and making the nav sticky on scroll

Solution 3: Simplify cross-franchise and cross-product navigation through an improved information hierarchy and contextual navigation
