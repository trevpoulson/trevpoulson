Product Design

I believe that when we understand our users' motivations, needs, and behaviors, we can enhance their lives through well designed products.

Design Process

Great products are created to solve problems — being able to articulate the problem, why it matters to our users, and establish clear KPIs is the first step in my design process.

Before design begins however, I work to discover the bigger picture by understanding the users, the competitive landscape, exsisting research, and stakeholders needs. By the end of the discovery phase, the team works together to identify How we should solve the problem with a set of hypotheses.

Armed with hypotheses, I involve the entire team in the design process through brainstorms, design reviews, and ad-hoc discussions. I strongly believe the best designs are discussed early and often with as many people as possible. Once a direction is decided upon, it is continually validated with users and iterated upon until the team is 80% confident it will solve the problems.

Now that we know What we're building, I work closely with the engineering team throughout the development process then A/B test the design to ensure it meets the established KPIs and goals. If all goes well, we launch the product.

After launch, it's important to continually monitor the KPIs and continue user research to understand where future improvment can be made.
